Research Portfolio

I design AI systems that empower rather than exploit vulnerable populations, focusing on equity-centered approaches in high-stakes contexts like mental health, dating, and humanitarian work. My research combines HCI methods with participatory design to create technology that respects users' lived realities and amplifies existing support structures.

Each project in this portfolio is a step toward that vision, grounded in fieldwork, participatory methods, and a commitment to equity-centered design.

Projects

AI for Humanitarian Negotiations

LLM-powered tools that enhance humanitarian negotiators' expertise in crisis zones.

  • Support established practices
  • Contextualize information
  • Enable validation processes

Methods: Field studies, interviews with frontline negotiators, design probing, prototype testing

LLMs for Mental Health Support

Investigating LLM chatbots for mental health support, particularly for LGBTQ+ individuals.

  • Benefits: accessibility, non-judgment
  • Challenges: harmful advice, unreliable support

Methods: Qualitative analysis of user experiences, interviews, comparative evaluation with therapy principles

Reducing Bias in Online Platforms

Designing interfaces that reduce implicit racial bias in dating platforms.

  • "Neutral" design choices can amplify racial bias
  • Cognitive forcing functions mitigate these biases

Methods: Online experiments, interface design, analysis of decision patterns
