Research

I design and implement equitable systems for healthcare and social applications to enhance the reliability and fairness of AI, improving outcomes for marginalized communities and advancing the ethical use of technology.

While earning my Ph.D. at Harvard University, my research includes interdisciplinary studies focusing on human-computer interaction, specifically designing equitable and interpretable machine learning systems for healthcare and social applications. Current projects involve developing systems for frontline humanitarian negotiation and evaluating large language models for mental health support, aimed at enhancing AI reliability and fairness. Additionally, I am preparing three different papers focusing on understanding the effects of voice assistants’ accents on AI over-reliance, designing AI tools for frontline humanitarian negotiators, and designing educational tools for LGBTQ+ allies to better advocate for LGBTQ+ rights.
