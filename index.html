I design intelligent systems to address social challenges faced by vulnerable populations, including racial minorities and LGBTQ+ communities. My research focuses on creating socio-technical solutions that amplify positive social forces and align with individual intentions to achieve lasting, impactful change.

My work spans multiple domains, including using LLMs to support humanitarian negotiators, evaluating AI-based mental health support for LGBTQ+ individuals, and designing interfaces that reduce racial bias in online platforms. I received a Best Student Paper Finalist award at the AMIA conference for my work on understanding LLM chatbots for mental well-being.

Through my research, I challenge the assumption that technology inherently drives positive social change, demonstrating how thoughtful design interventions can mitigate biases and empower communities with established practices to support their vulnerable members.
