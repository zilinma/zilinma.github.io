I design intelligent systems to address social challenges faced by vulnerable populations. My research creates socio-technical solutions that amplify positive social forces and align with user intentions for lasting impact.

My work spans using LLMs for humanitarian negotiation, evaluating AI mental health support, and reducing bias in online platforms. I received the AMIA Best Student Paper Finalist award for my research on LLM chatbots for mental well-being.

Featured Projects

AI for Humanitarian Negotiations

Designing LLM-powered tools that enhance rather than replace the expertise of humanitarian negotiators working in crisis zones. This research focuses on supporting established practices, contextualizing information, and enabling validation processes.

Methods: Field studies, interviews with frontline negotiators, design probing, prototype testing

View Project

LLMs for Mental Health Support

Investigating how LLM chatbots impact mental health support, particularly for LGBTQ+ individuals. This work reveals both benefits (accessibility, non-judgment) and challenges (harmful advice, unreliable support) of AI mental health tools.

Methods: Qualitative analysis of user experiences, interviews, comparative evaluation with therapy principles

View Project

Reducing Bias in Online Platforms

Designing interfaces that reduce implicit racial bias in decision-making. This work demonstrated how seemingly "neutral" design choices in dating platforms can amplify racial bias, and developed cognitive forcing functions to mitigate these biases.

Methods: Online experiments, interface design, analysis of decision patterns
